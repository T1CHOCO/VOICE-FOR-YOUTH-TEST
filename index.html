Citizen Emergency Pocket Guide

Emergency Minor Rights Card for Protests

Keep this emergency pocket checklist saved on your mobile device or print it. If a minor or student is stopped or questioned by police, these fundamental rights apply immediately under Indian law.

EMERGENCY JUVENILE RIGHTS CARD
Under Indian Juvenile Justice Act & Constitution Art. 21
1
No Lockup / Jail for Minors: Minors under 18 CANNOT be placed in a standard police lockup or jail. They must only be handled by a Child Welfare Officer or Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU).
2
No Force or Uniform Intimidation: Police personnel handling minors should ideally not wear uniforms or display weapons (JJ Rules 2016). Physical violence or lathi-charge against children is strictly illegal (Sec 75 JJ Act).
3
Immediate Parent & Lawyer Notification: Parents/Guardians and a legal aid lawyer must be informed immediately upon apprehension.
4
Right to Medical Examination: A complete medical checkup by a certified doctor is mandatory within 24 hours to document any injuries.
5
Bail is the Default Rule: Under Section 12 of JJ Act, bail is mandatory for juveniles regardless of bailable/non-bailable nature of the alleged offense.
